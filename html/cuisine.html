Cuisine

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a savory dish typically of marinated beef. It is often grilled with onions and garlic to make its flavors pop. Sirloin, rib eye, or brisket are the most common meats used for the dish. Bulgogi could also a spicy pork dish, but beef is more common.

Samgyeopsal

Samgyeopsal is one of South Korea’s most popular dishes. It consists of grilled slices of pork belly meat that are not marinated or seasoned. The meat often resembles short pieces of bacon. The dish is typically eaten by dipping the meat in a sauce made of salt and pepper in sesame seed oil and wrapped in lettuce along with grilled slices of garlic, grilled slices of onion, shredded green onions, and kimchi.

Japchae

Japchae is a sweet and flavorful traditional Korean noodle dish. It is often served as a side dish and not the main dish. It made of stir-fried sweet potato, thinly shredded vegetables, beef, and a hint of soy sauce and sugar. Depending on the chef’s preferences, it can also contain mushrooms.

Kimchi

Kimchi is one of the most essential dishes in Korean cuisine. Dinner is considered incomplete without it. Kimichi is a sour and spicy dish fermented vegetables. Though it is made with many different ingredients, the most common main ingredient is cabbage.

Bibimbap

Bibimbap translates to mixed rice with meat and vegetables. Ingredients include, but are not limited to, namul (Korean seasoned spinach), mushrooms, beef, soy sauce, gochujang (Korean chili pepper paste), rice, and a fried egg. More traditional and authentic versions of Bibimbap are made with raw beef and egg yolk.

Gimbap

Gimbap is (also spelled Kimbap) a popular on the go snack and also for school. It may look like sushi, but they are different dishes. Unlike Sushi, Gimbap does not use raw fish but instead cooked ingredients like kimchi, tuna, bulgogi, ham and cheese, and other vegetables. All the filling ingredients are then wrapped rice and dried seaweed.
