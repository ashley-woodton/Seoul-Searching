1Million Dacne Studio and Han River Moonlight Cruise

1Million Dance studio is easily the most recognized studio in Korea. Anyone interested in dance is welcomed to come. They offer everything from beginner classes to master classes. They even have a program called Step By 1Million for dance newcomers where one piece of choreography is taught in four classes instead of one. With instructors that choreograph according to their styles, no two classes are ever the same.The beast age range would probably be 12 - 45 yrs of age due to the some songs and choreography.

Prices:

  • 5 class: $93.17 (₩110,000)
  • 10 class: $169.40 (₩200,000)
  • 20 class: $271.05 (₩320,000)
  • 30 class: $304.93 (₩360,000)
  • 40 class: $338.81 (₩400,000)

See Seoul differently by taking a moonlight tour on the beautiful Han River that bisects Seoul. Enjoy the beautiful night view and live music as you cruise for 40 minutes. This tour is only for non-Korean nationals, so a valid ID is required for boarding. It leaves out every day, except for Mondays, from the Yeouido Han River Ferry Terminal near Yeouinaru Station. You can bring the whole family on this cruise but be aware the cruise starts at 9 pm so you may not want to bring your little ones. The tour is more enjoyable for ages 16 and up.

Prices:

  • Adult (ages 13+): $14.90 (₩17,618)
  • Child (ages 3 - 12: $10.40 (₩12,297)
  • Chirldren under 3 yrs of age are free
Private Best Food Toor and Gyeongbokgung Palace

Discover Korea through food! The Best Food Tour in Seoul is a private 4-hour walking tour with a local guide. During the tour, you will have 4 different meals and see the difference between ancient and modern Seoul. You'll even walk through hidden alleys only known by locals. The tour starts at 5 pm and requires a minimum of 3 travelers. This tour is best for ages 18 and up.

Price:

$89 per person (₩105,236)

Gyeongbokgung Palace was built in 1395 during the Joseon Dynasty. When you visit you'll get to see the throne hall and floating pavilion, the royal living quarters, and the library. There is also a ceremony that takes place when the change guards, at 10 am and 2 pm, that you will not want to miss! The palace is also the home National Folk Museum of Korea and the National Palace Museum of Korea. The best ages to visit this site would be ages 18+ yrs because they are old enough to really appreciate the history.

Admission Prices:

  • Adults (ages 19-64): $2.54 (₩3,000) / Groups (10 people or more): $2.03 (₩2,400)
  • Children (ages 7-18): $1.27 (₩1,500) / Groups (10 people or more): $1.02 (₩1,200)

Free admission:

  • Preschoolers (ages 6 and younger)
  • Seniors (ages 65 and older)
  • People wearing hanboks
  • On the last Wednesday of every month (Culture day)
Lotte World and Thanks Nature Cafe

Visit South Korea's version of Disney Land, Lotte World! Lotte World is a massive theme park and is home to the world's largest indoor theme park. The theme park is split into 2 parks: Magic Island and Adventure. Lotte World also has shopping malls, restaurants, a luxury hotel, bungee jumping, swimming pools, and a Korean folk museum. Just like Disney, Lotte World aims to target all ages by providing fun for all.

Prices:

  • Adult (ages 19+): $49.90 (₩59,000)
  • Youth (ages 13 - 18): $43.98 (₩52,000)
  • Senior (ages 65+) and Child (ages3 - 12): $39.75 (₩47,000)
  • Baby (ages 1 - 2: $12.69 (₩15,000)

Thanks Nature Cafe is one of the oldest animal cafes in Seoul. It is a waffle and coffee sheep cafe. There are two sections in the cafe. One where you can drink a beverage of your choice or try some dessert, while in the other you can meet, pet and feed some sheep. and They currently only have 2 lambs named Bently and William. The cafe is also ranked number 33 of the 2,027 coffee and tea places in Seoul. This cafe is located by Hongik University’s campus and is in the trendy Hongdae. It's clear to see their target consumers are teen students and young adults.

Prices will vary.
